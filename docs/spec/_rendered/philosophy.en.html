ATS Philosophy — Why a New Time Standard

The Gregorian calendar is a farming tool from the 16th century. ATS is a cognitive tool for a multi-planetary civilization.

ATS is not adopted on astronomical superiority — it is adopted on biological and cognitive fit. The case rests on four cycles where Gregorian falls short and ATS proposes a cleaner alternative.

1. The Circadian Cycle — Deep Work Above the Hour

Gregorian problem. The 60-minute hour is an industrial artefact. Too long for a micro-task, too short to enter focused flow. It fragments attention.

ATS proposal — the Bloc (0.1 day ≈ 2h 24min).

  • Aligns with ultradian rhythms of the human brain (90–120 minutes of neural intensity, followed by recovery).
  • A working day stops being "8 hours" and becomes "3 or 4 Blocs of focus" — a unit of cognitive performance, not raw duration.

ATS does not measure time-that-passes; it measures attention available.

2. The Social Cycle — The Deka (10 days)

Gregorian problem. The 7-day week (5 work / 2 rest) leaves a recovery ratio of ~28%, often insufficient against modern burnout — a rhythm inherited from religious constraints, not human design.

ATS proposal — the Deka.

ATS does not legislate a work-rest split inside the Deka (this is explicitly out of scope, see manifesto §11). However, the ten-day unit naturally invites richer rhythms than 5+2 — for instance, communities may experiment with:

  • 7 days of action / 3 days of regeneration (30% rest)
  • 6+1+2+1 (anti-burnout split)
  • 8+2 (light variant, closest to current weekend)

The choice is social, not specified by the standard.

3. The Project Cycle — The End of the "Vague Month"

Gregorian problem. Months are irregular (28, 30, 31 days). Comparing February to March introduces ~10% statistical noise into every aggregated metric on Earth.

ATS proposal — the Hecto (100 days).

  • A clean decimal unit.
  • Roadmaps: "We ship in 1 Hecto" (concrete) replaces "by Q3" (fuzzy).
  • Wellness programs: "100-day reset" already exists colloquially — ATS makes it a canonical unit.

The Hecto is the gold standard of project cadence in a data-driven economy.

4. The Vital Cycle — The Three Ages of Life

Gregorian problem. We count age in solar years, an arbitrary increment with no intrinsic narrative. Turning "30" or "40" creates contrived anxiety thresholds.

ATS proposal — the Generation (~10 000 days ≈ 27.4 years), an informal unit.

ATS structures human life into three narrative acts:

Era Days Years Phase
0 → 1 Generation 0 — 10 000 0 — 27 Learning — brain plasticity, exploration
1 → 2 Generations 10 000 — 20 000 27 — 54 Building — career, family, ambition
2 → 3 Generations 20 000 — 30 000 54 — 82 Transmission — mastery, mentorship

Crossing a Generation is a rite of passage, not a decline. You don't age — you change Era.

Note. "Generation" is not a positional digit in the canonical ATS format. It lives entirely in social/philosophical discourse. The format keeps Kilo unbounded; "1 Generation" simply means "10 Kilos elapsed since birth".

5. Proposed Rituals

5.1 Kilo-versary (~ every 2.74 years)

Every 1 000 days lived, a personal review:

  • What was accomplished this Kilo?
  • What is the intent for the next?

More meaningful than a yearly birthday and rare enough to be a real checkpoint.

5.2 Hecto-celebration (~ every 100 days)

A lighter quarterly ritual: a personal or team milestone, often used for retrospectives, wellness check-ins, or project closes.

5.3 Generation transitions

Crossing 1 Generation (age 27) — entry into Building. Crossing 2 Generations (age 54) — entry into Transmission.

A society adopting ATS may attach civic rituals to these transitions.

The Gregorian calendar endures the time of Nature. ATS organizes the time of Consciousness.
