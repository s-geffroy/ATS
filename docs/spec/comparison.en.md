# ATS — Comparison with Alternative Calendars

Six reform proposals are reviewed below, organized in three families: solar optimization, decimal rupture, and exotic / multi-planetary.

---

## Family 1 — Solar Optimization (improve the Gregorian)

| System | Concept | vs ATS | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **International Fixed Calendar (IFC)** | 13 months of 28 days + 1 blank day. Same date → same weekday. | Fixes weekday alignment but keeps months and years (regulatory noise). ATS removes both. | IFC is a better past; ATS is the next system. |
| **Hanke-Henry Permanent** | 364-day year with leap-week every 5–6 years. Abolishes time zones. | Shares zone abolition but reintroduces irregularity via leap weeks. | Too gymnastic for the public. |
| **Holocene Calendar (HE)** | Add 10 000 years to Gregorian (2026 → 12026). Origin: agriculture. | Cosmetic: better for archaeology, no daily improvement. | HE is paint; ATS is structure. |

---

## Family 2 — Decimal Rupture (base 10 like ATS)

| System | Concept | vs ATS | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **French Republican (1793)** | 12 months × 30 days, 10-day weeks. Failed: too political, 1 rest day out of 10. | ATS learns from the failure: the Deka does not legislate a rhythm, leaving 7+3 or other splits open. | Republican burned out the workers; ATS leaves rest as a social choice. |
| **Swatch Internet Time (1998)** | Day in 1000 `.beats`. No time zones. | Only handled the clock, not dates — a marketing layer on top of Gregorian. ATS unifies date and clock in one number. | Gadget; ATS is a full system. |

---

## Family 3 — Exotic / Multi-Planetary

| System | Concept | vs ATS | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Darian Calendar (Mars)** | 24 Martian months, 7-day weeks (sols), tuned to Martian seasons. | Darian is *local* (only useful on Mars). ATS is *universal* — same instant on Earth, Moon, Mars. | Use Darian for Martian agriculture; ATS for Earth-Mars communication. |

---

## Strategic positioning

ATS has no direct competitor because it combines three philosophies:

1. **Mayan arithmetic** — time as linear accumulation (Long Count), not cyclic return.
2. **NASA engineering** — Point Zero (1969) and T+/T- notation rooted in spaceflight culture.
3. **Cognitive ergonomics** — Bloc/Deka/Hecto sized to human focus, rest and planning windows.
